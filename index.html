Pothos Care Guide

Soil for Pothos

Well-draining, all-purpose mix.

Pothos plants thrive in well-draining soil, such as a standard potting mix. Ensure good aeration for healthy root development.

Watering

Moderate watering; allow topsoil to dry.

Water Pothos when the topsoil feels slightly dry. They tolerate some drought but prefer consistent moisture without waterlogging.

Lighting

Indirect to low light conditions.

Place Pothos in locations with indirect to low light. They can adapt to various light conditions but avoid direct sunlight to prevent leaf burn.

Benefits

Air-purifying qualities.

Pothos is known for its ability to purify indoor air by removing common pollutants, contributing to a healthier living space.

Easy to propagate and maintain.

These plants are easy to propagate and maintain, making them suitable for beginners and plant enthusiasts of all levels.

Non-Benefits

Overwatering can lead to root rot; ensure proper drainage. Not suitable for bright, direct sunlight for extended periods.
